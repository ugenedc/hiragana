Study Japanese Anywhere: Tips for Learning While Traveling

April 8, 2024
Study Tips
By Eugene D'Hage-Craig

Whether you're on a business trip, vacation, or just commuting, maintaining your Japanese studies can be challenging. However, with the right approach and tools, you can turn travel time into productive learning time. Here's how to keep up with your Japanese studies while on the move.

1. Make Use of Dead Time

Travel often involves periods of waiting or transit that can be perfect for language learning:

  • Practice Hiragana during flight takeoff and landing when electronics are restricted
  • Review vocabulary while waiting for transportation
  • Use commute time for listening practice
  • Turn airport delays into study sessions

2. Leverage Mobile Apps

Mobile apps are your best friends for studying on the go:

  • Use Kanabloom for quick Hiragana and Katakana practice sessions
  • Download offline content for studying without internet
  • Set up notifications to remind you to practice
  • Use cloud sync to keep your progress updated across devices

3. Create a Portable Study Kit

Prepare a lightweight study kit that you can easily carry:

  • Small notebook for writing practice
  • Flashcards for offline review
  • Downloaded study materials on your device
  • Portable power bank to keep your devices charged

4. Set Realistic Goals

Adjust your study goals to match your travel schedule:

  • Focus on quality over quantity
  • Break study sessions into smaller chunks
  • Set achievable daily targets
  • Track your progress to stay motivated

5. Make It a Travel Game

Turn your travel experiences into learning opportunities:

  • Practice reading Japanese signs and advertisements you encounter
  • Learn vocabulary related to your travel activities
  • Document your journey in simple Japanese sentences
  • Challenge yourself to use Japanese when possible

6. Stay Connected

Maintain your learning momentum through connectivity:

  • Join online Japanese learning communities
  • Share your progress on social media
  • Connect with language exchange partners virtually
  • Use video chat for remote tutoring sessions

Conclusion

Travel doesn't have to interrupt your Japanese learning journey. With these strategies and the right tools like Kanabloom, you can maintain and even accelerate your progress while on the move. Remember, consistency is key – even short study sessions add up over time.
