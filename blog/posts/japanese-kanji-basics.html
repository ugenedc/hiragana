Japanese Kanji Basics: A Beginner's Guide

April 12, 2024
Kanji
By Eugene D'Hage-Craig

Kanji is one of the three writing systems used in Japanese, alongside Hiragana and Katakana. Here's a beginner's guide to understanding and learning Kanji:

1. What is Kanji?

Kanji are logographic characters borrowed from Chinese. Each character represents a word or a part of a word. There are thousands of Kanji characters, but you only need to learn about 2,000 to be literate in Japanese.

2. Structure of Kanji

Kanji characters are made up of radicals, which are smaller components that give clues to the meaning or pronunciation of the character. Learning common radicals can help you understand and remember Kanji more easily.

3. How to Learn Kanji

Start with basic Kanji that are commonly used in everyday life. Use flashcards, apps, or books to practice writing and recognizing characters. Focus on learning the meaning, pronunciation, and stroke order of each character.

4. Practice Regularly

Consistency is key when learning Kanji. Set aside time each day to practice writing and reading Kanji. Use them in sentences to reinforce your understanding.

5. Use Resources

There are many resources available to help you learn Kanji, including online courses, apps, and books. Find the ones that work best for you and stick with them.

By following these tips, you'll be well on your way to mastering Kanji. Happy learning!
